# Valhalla Productions Knowledge Base

## 1. Organizational Identity & Brand Architecture

### Corporate Structure
- **Valhalla Productions LLC**: Core operations and management entity (rebranded from Wrestling Tournament Pro)
- **Valhalla Wrestling**: Overarching public-facing brand identity, centered on "Warrior" ethos
- **Valhalla Wrestling Series**: Standardized event circuit (the "Brawl" series) for consistent high-quality experience

### Brand Identity
- **Origin Story**: Viking/Warrior motif tied to series inception at Viking Hall in Bristol, TN
- **Naming Convention**: All events use "Brawl" naming (e.g., Roanoke Brawl, State Line Brawl, Dayton Brawl)
- **Typography**: Hudson NY Serif
- **Visual Elements**: Spears, shields, Viking helmets integrated into awards and venue

### Brand Mission
Deliver "national-level" tournament experience—typically reserved for elite, high-cost championships—to regional families. Professional execution over operational shortcuts.

## 2. The Valhalla Promise (Core Principles)

### Five Non-Negotiable Promises
1. **Punctuality**: Events start on time, every time, no exception
2. **Efficiency**: Individual sessions engineered to run in under 3 hours
3. **National-Level Structure**: Defined weight classes, certified officials, 15-point technical falls, full-length matches
4. **Technological Superiority**: Exclusive use of TrackWrestling for digital scoring
5. **Experience Leadership**: Best tournament experience in youth wrestling market

### Operational Decision Filters
- **Backward Planning**: Build logistics in reverse from advertised start time
- **Communication Excellence**: Proactive messaging via digital and physical channels
- **The "Trust Metric"**: Mat flow is primary indicator—mats must stay active

## 3. Tournament Structure & Technical Specs

### Participant Divisions (2026-27 Standard)
| Division | Birthdate Range | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| 6U | 01/26/2019 – 01/25/2026 | No High Schoolers |
| 8U | 01/26/2017 – 01/25/2026 | No High Schoolers |
| 10U | 01/26/2015 – 01/25/2026 | No High Schoolers |
| 12U | 01/26/2013 – 01/25/2026 | No High Schoolers |
| 15U | 01/26/2010 – 01/25/2026 | No HS; Max 8th Grade |
| High School | Enrolled HS Students | When offered |

### Bracketing Rules
- **6 or fewer wrestlers**: Round Robin (min 3 matches)
- **8–64 wrestlers**: Double Elimination
- **Match Standards**: 15-point technical falls, full-length matches, no split mats
- **Seeding**: State Champion, State Placer, Region Champion criteria

### Weight Class Philosophy
- Uses **Defined Weight Classes** (not Madison Style)
- One-pound allowance for singlet/scale variance

### Weigh-In Options
1. **Standard On-Site**: Evening-before or morning-of
2. **Satellite Verification**: Official weights from prior Saturday tournaments
3. **Virtual Weigh-In**: Video-verified remote option

## 5. Target Audience & Tourism Model

### Primary Personas
- **Parents/Wrestlers**: Focus on Time ROI (3-hour sessions) and tangible rewards
- **Coaches/Club Leaders**: High-level competition, accurate seeding, stress-free logistics
- **Tourism Partners**: Economic impact and repeat visitation

### Tourism Economic Model
- **Spectator Multiplier**: 600+ athletes → 1,500–1,700+ spectators (2.5x–3x)
- **Geographic Draw**: 150-mile primary radius
- **Overnight Stay**: 20% of families
- **Repeat Visitation**: Series points system encourages multiple stops

## 6. Managed Services & Host Partnerships

### Three-Tier Service Model
1. **Turnkey Production**: Full management (marketing to 10,000+ coaches, TrackWrestling, staffing, equipment)
2. **Tournament Operator**: Day-of operations, seeding, bracket management
3. **Remote Limited Support**: TrackWrestling consultation and troubleshooting

### Host Requirements
- Venue: 6+ mats, 500+ spectators
- Hosts provide: Table workers, basic setup/mats
- Receive: Fundraising fee based on participation
