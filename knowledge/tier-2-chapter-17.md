# Chapter 17: Running a Dual Tournament

## What Is a Dual Tournament?

A dual tournament is team-vs-team competition where two teams face off in a scheduled match format. Unlike bracket tournaments (single/double elimination), duals are scored on match wins.

### Format Overview

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Teams** | Typically 8-16 teams |
| **Format** | Each team fields wrestlers at each weight class |
| **Scoring** | Most match wins wins the dual |
| **Duration** | 3-5 hours depending on team size |

---

## Key Differences from Bracket Tournaments

### Scoring Structure

| Bracket Tournament | Dual Tournament |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Individual placement | Team score |
| Medal awards | Team placement |
| Single elimination possible | Full lineup required |
| Seeding important | Matchups matter |

### Match Management

- Every match counts for team score
- Team lineup must be submitted
- Weight classes matter more
- Cannot scratch easily (affects team)

---

## Setting Up a Dual Tournament

### Pre-Event Requirements

1. **Team Registration**
   - Collect team registrations
   - Require lineup in advance
   - Know weight classes each team will fill

2. **Venue Setup**
   - Mats in central area
   - Team seating areas
   - Scoring table with PA
   - Scoreboard visible to all

3. **Schedule**
   - Round-robin or bracket format
   - Typically 1 day for duals
   - Set start times (9 AM standard)

---

## Dual Tournament Scoring

### Standard Scoring

| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Win by pin | 6 points |
| Win by tech fall (15+) | 5 points |
| Major decision (8-14) | 4 points |
| Decision (1-7) | 3 points |
| Loss | 0 points |

### Dual Winner

Team with most points after all matches wins the dual.

Alternative: Best-of series (wrestle 3, win 2)

---

## Team Management

### Pre-Dual Communication

- Confirm teams attending
- Collect lineup from each coach
- Verify weights
- Set schedule for weigh-ins

### Day-of Procedures

- Team check-in
- Weigh-ins (may differ from bracket events)
- Team lineup submission
- Bracket/bracket generation
- Scoring updates after each round

---

## Special Considerations for Duals

### Venue Space

Duals need less space than big bracket tournaments, but more team areas:
- Bleacher space for teams
- Warm-up area for each team
- Coaching area

### Timing

Duals typically run faster:
- Quick matches
- No long breaks between rounds
- Set schedule per round

### Awards

- Team awards (1st, 2nd, 3rd)
- Individual awards may vary
- Often simpler than bracket events

---

## Summary: Duals

| Aspect | Key Point |
|--------|-----------|

| Venue | Needs team seating areas |
