### Step 1: Work Backward from Start Time

Tournament starts at 10 AM. Here's an example formula:

| Time | Task |
|------|------|
| 10:00 AM | First match on ALL mats |
| 9:50 AM | Mats cleared, final announcements, opening ceremony |
| 9:30 AM | Brackets finalized, Bouts posted, Start time reminder, Officials/Staff meeting |
| 9:15 AM | Coaches meeting |
| 9:00 AM | Brackets posted, Officials arrive, Start time reminder announcement |
| 8:35 AM | Bracket adjustments |
| 8:30 AM | Weigh-in closed |
| 7:30 AM | Doors open, weigh-in begins |
| 7:00 AM | Staff arrives, equipment check |

**Is your tournament schedule mapped in detail? If not, you NEED our system.**

---

### Step 2: The Mat Flow Principle

Empty mats = long days.

**Goal:** Every mat should have a match within 1 minute of the previous match concluding.

---

### Step 3: The Communication Standard

Parents & Coaches don't overwhelm staff with questions when they know the answers.

**Pro tip:** Tell them what they need to know before they need to know it.
