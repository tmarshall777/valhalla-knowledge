# Chapter 16: Long-Term Analysis

## Getting Better Every Year

Your first event won't be your best. Your 50th should be. Here's how to continuously improve.

---

## Metrics to Track

### Every Year, Track:

| Metric | How to Measure | Goal |
|--------|---------------|------|
| **Registration count** | TrackWrestling total | Growing |
| **Return rate** | (Year 2 attendees ÷ Year 1) | 80%+ |
| **New attendees** | First-time registrants | Growing |
| **Net promoter score** | Survey question | 70%+ |
| **Start time accuracy** | Planned vs. actual | 100% on time |
| **Revenue per event** | Total revenue | Growing |
| **Profit margin** | (Revenue - Costs) ÷ Revenue | 30%+ |

---

## Year-Over-Year Comparison

### Create an Annual Report

Each year, compile:

1. **Registration trends**
2. **Revenue growth**
3. **Profitability changes**
4. **What worked**
5. **What didn't work**

### Questions to Answer

- Are we growing?
- Are we profitable?
- Are families returning?
- Are we improving operations?

---

## The Improvement Cycle

```
Year 1 → Track metrics → Identify issues → Fix for Year 2 → Track → Adjust → Year 3
```

Each event should be better than the last.

---

## Updating Your Playbook

After each event, update your:

- [ ] Checklists (based on what was missed)
- [ ] Templates (based on what didn't work)
- [ ] Staff training (based on issues)
- [ ] Communication (based on feedback)
- [ ] Venue selection (if issues)

Your playbook grows more valuable every year.

---

## Summary: Long-Term

- Track key metrics annually
- Compare year over year
- Fix what didn't work
- Build your playbook
- Never stop improving
