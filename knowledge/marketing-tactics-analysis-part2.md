## New Tactics Discovered

### 1. The Sales Process Analogy (Powerful!)
**What they do:**
- "The Job Search is a Sales Process"
- "YOU = The Product"
- "Employer = The Buyer"
- "Resume = Marketing Brochure"
- "Interview = The Sales Call"

**Why it works:** Makes abstract job search concrete and actionable

**Valhalla adaptation:**
- "Running a Tournament is a Service Business"
- "Your Tournament = The Product"
- "Parents/Coaches = The Customers"
- "Their Problem = Disorganized Events"
- "Your Solution = On-Time, Professional Experience"

### 4. Interview Prep Structure (50 Tough Questions)
**What they do:**
- "50 Tough Questions – ANSWERED!"
- Anticipate objections
- Provide ready answers

**Valhalla adaptation:**
- "50 Questions Tournament Directors Ask – ANSWERED!"
- "FAQ: Running Your First Tournament"
- Preemptively address:
  - "How much does it cost?"
  - "How do I get refs?"
  - "What if weigh-ins run late?"

### 7. Multiple Touch Message Ideas
**What they do:**
- Ask if they will forward your resume
- Ask for a referral
- Let them know about a course you completed
- Send a video clip
- Scan and send your brag book
- Send a 30/60/90-Day Plan

**Valhalla multiple touch ideas:**
- First touch: Free checklist
- Second touch: "Did you try this tip?"
- Third touch: "Here's a mistake to avoid"
- Fourth touch: "Our Operations Guide just launched"
- Fifth touch: "Special offer — 50% off"

### Messaging Structure

**Email Subject Lines:**
- [Result]: "Run Events That Start On Time"
- [Question]: "Why Do Your Tournaments Always Run Late?"
- [Story]: "The Mom Who Left at 8PM"
- [Stat]: "80% of Tournaments Start Late — Here's Why"

**Email Body Formula:**
1. Hook (pain or curiosity)
2. Story or stat
3. Solution preview
4. Call to action
