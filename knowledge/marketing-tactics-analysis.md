# Marketing Tactics Analysis
## Learning from Career Confidential (Peggy McKee)

Based on the 10 PDFs Tim shared, here's what makes their marketing effective — and how we can adapt it for Valhalla.

---

## Key Tactics Used by Career Confidential

### 1. Branded Methodology Name
**What they do:** "Hidden Jobs Finder" — not just "job search tips"
**Valhalla adaptation:** "The On-Time System" or "Valhalla Tournament Framework"

---

### 2. Big Stat Hook
**What they do:** "70-80% of all jobs are not posted online"
**Valhalla adaptation:** "90% of youth wrestling tournaments start late" (need to verify this or similar)

---

### 3. Presenter Authority Bio
**What they do:**
- "Over 22,000 hours of recruiting experience"
- "10 years" / "20+ years"
- "Fortune 500 clients" (GE Healthcare, Bayer, Roche, BD, Qiagen)
- Specific, quantified expertise

**Valhalla adaptation:**
- "100+ events run"
- "20+ years running tournaments"
- "Every Valhalla event starts on time — guaranteed"

---

### 4. The "Club" Model
**What they do:** "Career Confidential Coaching Club"
- Creates community
- Implies ongoing value
- Membership tier structure

**Valhalla adaptation:** "Valhalla Tournament Directors Academy" or "Valhalla Partners"

---

### 5. Numbered Tip Structure
**What they do:** "17 Tips to Write a Cover Letter"
- Easy to digest
- Shareable
- Feels actionable

**Valhalla adaptation:** "5 Steps to an On-Time Tournament" or "7 Secrets Tournament Directors Need to Know"

---

### 6. Problem/Solution Framework
**What they do:**
- "You spend hours online competing with thousands" (pain)
- "Go around all those other job seekers straight to the hiring manager" (solution)

**Valhalla adaptation:**
- "You paid $50, drove 90 minutes, kid wrestled twice in 8 hours" (pain)
- "Our system guarantees start on time, ends in 3 hours" (solution)

---

### 7. Multiple Touch / Follow-Up Sequence
**What they do:** "The Concept of Multiple Touch" — reach out repeatedly
**Valhalla adaptation:** Email nurture sequence (5 emails after lead magnet)

---

### 8. "The Rules" as Credibility Builder
**What they do:** "The Rules" section — simple rules the reader must follow
**Valhalla adaptation:** "The 3 Rules Every Tournament Director Must Follow"

---

### 9. FAQ Section
**What they do:** Preempt objections before they arise
**Valhalla adaptation:** "Frequently Asked Questions About Running On-Time Tournaments"

---

### 10. Action-Oriented Section Titles
**What they do:** "Finding a Market for Your Product", "How to Find Hundreds of Hiring Managers"
**Valhalla adaptation:** "How to Set Up TrackWrestling in 30 Minutes", "How to Build Your Brackets"

---

### 11. Teaser + Full System Ladder
**What they do:**
- Free guide (teaser)
- Webinar (more depth)
- Full course (complete system)
- Coaching club (premium)

**Valhalla adaptation:**
- Tier 1: Free checklist (we have this)
- Tier 2: Operations Guide ($67-97)
- Tier 3: Templates/Toolkit ($147-197)
- Tier 4: Video Masterclass ($347-497)
- Tier 5: VIP Support ($1,497)

---

### 12. Social Proof Through Specificity
**What they do:** List specific clients: "GE HealthCare, Bayer Diagnostics, Roche Diagnostics, BD, Qiagen"
**Valhalla adaptation:** "We've run events for Bill Brimer Brawl in the Hall, Ed Cressel Classic, VHSL/TSSAA District Tournaments"

---

## Structure Templates They Use

### Webinar Structure (Standard):
1. Today's Agenda
2. Introduction of Presenter (with credentials)
3. Presentation of the Webinar
4. Questions & Answers
5. Additional Resources
6. Feedback
7. How to Contact Us
8. Accessing the Archives

**Use for:** Our future webinars

### Guide Structure:
1. Introduction + Problem
2. The Rules
3. Why It Works (theory)
4. How To (practical steps)
5. Frequently Asked Questions
6. Key Ideas (Wrap Up)

**Use for:** Tier 2 Operations Guide
