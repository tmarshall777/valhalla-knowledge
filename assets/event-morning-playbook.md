## HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

- Print it. Don't try to run event morning off your phone.
- Work backward from *your* first whistle. Every T-minus time below is measured from that moment.
- Check boxes as you go. If you can't check a box, don't move on — stop and fix it.
- If you hit a problem you can't solve in under 5 minutes, escalate. Morning is not the time to troubleshoot alone.

## PART 1 — T-MINUS 5:00 HOURS | THE NIGHT-BEFORE CARRYOVER

*(Because morning execution actually starts the night before. This is the 10-minute check that prevents 90% of morning disasters.)*

**The night-before carryover checklist:**

☐ **Phone fully charged + backup battery pack in your bag**
*Why this matters: You will use your phone more on event day than any other day of the year. A dead phone at 8 AM is a dead event.*

☐ **Venue access confirmed for your arrival time (not doors-open time)**
*Why this matters: The #1 thing that kills a 7 AM arrival is a locked building and a facility contact who isn't answering. Confirm in writing the night before, not the morning of.*

☐ **Final bracket sheets printed (2 sets minimum) and in your bag**
*Why this matters: When TrackWrestling goes down — and at some point, it will — the paper brackets are the only thing standing between you and chaos.*

☐ **Staff roster printed with phone numbers**
*Why this matters: You need to be able to call a missing ref from the parking lot without scrolling through your contacts.*

☐ **Cash box / change / payment device ready**

☐ **Emergency contact sheet (EMT, venue manager, backup ref) in your bag**

☐ **Alarm set — AND a backup alarm on a second device**
*Why this matters: You cannot afford to oversleep. A single backup alarm turns a career-ending mistake into a near-miss.*

☐ **Outfit laid out — Valhalla (or your brand) gear visible to staff and families**
*Why this matters: On event morning, people need to find you fast. Brand gear is a visibility tool, not a vanity play.*

## PART 2 — T-MINUS 3:00 HOURS (7:00 AM) | ARRIVAL & VENUE ACTIVATION

*(You arrive 3 hours before first whistle. Not 2. Not 2.5. Three. This is non-negotiable.)*

**On arrival (first 15 minutes):**

☐ **Walk the full venue before you touch anything**
*Look for: temperature, lighting, smells, obvious damage, anything that wasn't the way you left it the night before. A 5-minute walkthrough catches problems before they become emergencies.*

☐ **Confirm all doors you need are unlocked or have a plan to be**
*Main entrance, loading dock, staff entrance, ref/staff room, weigh-in area. If any of these aren't sorted, that's your next 10 minutes.*

☐ **Power check: every outlet you need is live**
*Score tables, laptops, TrackWrestling, PA system, scoreboard, registration table. Test each one. Don't assume.*

☐ **Wi-Fi check: log in and run a speed test**
*Minimum viable: 25 Mbps down. If it's slower, you need a hotspot backup ready. TrackWrestling does not forgive a dead connection.*

☐ **Restrooms stocked, clean, and accessible**
*Not your job? It is this morning.*

☐ **HVAC confirmed working and set appropriately**
*Wrestlers cut weight. A hot venue is a dangerous venue.*

**Equipment positioning (next 30 minutes):**

☐ **Mats in final position, spacing verified, tape down**

☐ **Score tables positioned at every mat with chairs**

☐ **Head table set with all bracket sheets, laptops, and backup paper**

☐ **Weigh-in station set up with scale(s), wristbands, and sign-in sheets**

☐ **Registration/check-in desk staffed and ready**

☐ **Awards table staged (doesn't need to be out yet, but positioned for quick setup)**

☐ **Signage placed: parking, entrance, weigh-ins, registration, restrooms**
*Why this matters: Every piece of signage is a parent question you don't have to answer later.*

☐ **PA system tested with an actual announcement**
*Don't just tap the mic. Say a full sentence at full volume from your actual head table position.*

## PART 3 — T-MINUS 2:30 HOURS (7:30 AM) | STAFF ARRIVAL & MEETING

*(Your staff should arrive 2.5 hours before first whistle. If they don't, you have a staffing problem, not a timing problem.)*

**Staff check-in (15 minutes):**

☐ **Every staff member checked in and confirmed present**
*Use your printed roster. Physically check them off.*

☐ **Any no-shows identified and backup plan activated within 5 minutes**
*The first 5 minutes after a no-show are the most important. Every minute you wait, your options shrink.*

☐ **Staff credentials distributed (lanyards, wristbands, shirts)**

☐ **Staff directed to their assigned position — not just told, physically walked**
*Why this matters: "Table 3 is over there" is not an instruction. "Let me show you" is.*

**The staff meeting (15 minutes, non-negotiable):**

☐ **Welcome and thank-you (90 seconds)**
*This sets the tone for the entire day. Don't skip it.*

☐ **Today's schedule reviewed in detail**
*Start time, session breaks, scheduled end time. Everyone should be able to repeat it back.*

☐ **Position assignments confirmed out loud**
*"John, you're on Mat 2 as scorer. Sarah, you're running for Mats 1-3. Mike, you're floor manager."*

☐ **The one rule: every question goes to the floor manager first**
*Why this matters: This is how you keep yourself from being the bottleneck.*

☐ **Break schedule announced**
*People perform better when they know when their break is coming.*

☐ **Emergency procedures reviewed (medical, power, weather)**
*30 seconds each. Not a drill — just a refresher.*

☐ **"Who has questions?" — and actually wait for answers**
*Silence here means either total clarity or total confusion. Don't assume which.*

☐ **"Let's have a great event." — and mean it**

## PART 4 — T-MINUS 2:00 HOURS (8:00 AM) | DOORS OPEN & WEIGH-INS BEGIN

*(The moment the doors open, you lose control of the crowd. The only way to keep control of the event is to have every system running before they walk in.)*

**Before doors open (5-minute pre-flight):**

☐ **Every staff member at their position**

☐ **Weigh-in station fully operational — certified scale zeroed and tested**

☐ **Registration desk ready with check-in materials**

☐ **Head table has final brackets, staff roster, and schedule in hand**

☐ **Floor manager has walkie-talkie or equivalent, tested**

☐ **You have done one final bathroom run — you won't get another chance for 4 hours**

**Doors open — weigh-in protocol:**

☐ **Weigh-ins running with posted wait time (not just a line)**
*Why this matters: "You're 10 minutes out" is information. A line is anxiety.*

☐ **Wristbands applied immediately after successful weigh-in**
*Don't wait. Wristband is the proof-of-weight that prevents disputes at 9:30.*

☐ **Weight disputes routed to floor manager, not handled at the scale**

☐ **Missed-weight wrestlers logged and flagged for bracket adjustment**

☐ **Scratches logged in TrackWrestling immediately — not on paper for later**

**The 8:30 benchmark — ask yourself these questions:**

☐ **Are weigh-ins running on time, or are you already building a backlog?**
*If there's a backlog at 8:30, you either need to add a second scale or extend weigh-ins by 10 minutes. Decide now.*

☐ **Are any divisions in danger of not making their bracket lockdown?**

☐ **Have any staff issues emerged that need your attention?**

## PART 5 — T-MINUS 1:00 HOUR (9:00 AM) | THE LOCKDOWN SEQUENCE

*(This is the hardest hour of the morning. Everything that was loose now gets locked.)*

☐ **9:00 AM — Final weigh-in call announced over PA**
*Exact language: "Weigh-ins close in 30 minutes. If you are not weighed in by 9:30, you will not wrestle. This is your final call."*

☐ **9:00 AM — Officials arrive, check in, receive credentials**

☐ **9:00 AM — Brackets posted in public viewing area**

☐ **9:15 AM — Coaches meeting (5 minutes, not 15)**
*What to cover: any rule interpretations specific to today, bracket change protocol, how to handle disputes. That's it.*

☐ **9:30 AM — WEIGH-INS CLOSE. Hard stop.**
*The only thing harder than closing weigh-ins on time is explaining why your event started at 10:17.*

☐ **9:30 AM — Scratch deadline. Anyone not weighed in is out.**

☐ **9:30 AM — Bracket adjustments begin (floor manager leads)**

☐ **9:45 AM — Final brackets posted and distributed to mats**

☐ **9:50 AM — "First call" announcement over PA**
*"Wrestlers, please report to your assigned mats. First match in 10 minutes."*

☐ **9:55 AM — Officials in position at mats**

☐ **9:55 AM — Runners at staging area with first-match wrestlers ready**

☐ **9:58 AM — Head table confirms all mats are ready**
*If any mat is not ready at 9:58, you have 2 minutes to fix it or make a decision.*

☐ **10:00 AM — FIRST WHISTLE ON ALL MATS SIMULTANEOUSLY**
*This is the promise. This is what every minute of the last five hours was for.*

## THE MORNING REPORT CARD

*(Five minutes after first whistle, run this quick self-audit. It's the difference between a good morning and a learning opportunity.)*

Answer honestly:

☐ **Did first whistle happen at the scheduled time? (Not "close." On time.)**

☐ **Did all mats start simultaneously?**

☐ **Were weigh-ins closed exactly on time?**

☐ **Did every staff member show up and get into position without scrambling?**

☐ **Did the staff meeting happen and run on schedule?**

☐ **Were parents and coaches informed at every step, or did they have to ask?**

☐ **Did you handle any issues calmly, or were you reactive?**

☐ **Is the head table running smoothly, or is there already a line of questions?**

**Scoring:**
- **8 out of 8:** You ran a professional morning. This is the standard.
- **6–7 out of 8:** You're close. Small adjustments will get you there.
- **5 or below:** You have a system problem, not an effort problem. Keep reading.
