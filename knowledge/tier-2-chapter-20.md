# Chapter 20: Budgeting & Pricing

## The Bottom Line

A tournament that doesn't make money won't survive. This chapter shows you how to price for profit — without charging families too much.

---

## Understanding Your Costs

### Fixed Costs (Don't Change with Attendance)

| Cost | Typical Amount |
|------|----------------|
| Venue rental | $1,500-3,000 |
| Insurance | $200-500 |
| TrackWrestling | $150-300 |
| Awards (base order) | $800-1,500 |
| Marketing | $100-300 |
| **Total Fixed** | $2,750-5,600 |

### Variable Costs (Scale with Attendance)

| Cost | Per Unit |
|------|----------|
| Referee pay | $20-30/hour |
| Table worker pay | $15-20/hour |
| Extra awards | $3-5/wrestler |
| Food for staff | $100-200 |
| **Total Variable** | ~$10-15/wrestler |

---

## The Break-Even Formula

```
Break-Even = Fixed Costs ÷ (Entry Fee - Variable Cost per Wrestler)
```

### Example

- Fixed costs: $4,000
- Entry fee: $55
- Variable per wrestler: $12

```
Break-Even = $4,000 ÷ ($55 - $12) = 93 wrestlers
```

**What it means:** You need 93 wrestlers just to break even. Below that, you lose money.

## Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Price Point | When to Use |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Early Bird** | $45-50 | First 50-100 registrants |
| **Regular** | $55-65 | Standard pricing |
| **Day-of** | $75-85 | Limited spots, last minute |

### Factors That Affect Pricing

- **Regional norms:** What do similar events charge?
- **Value provided:** Are you providing premium experience?
- **Your costs:** Know your break-even first
- **Market position:** Premium events can charge more

### Profit Calculation

```
Revenue - Costs = Profit

Example:
300 wrestlers × $55 = $16,500
+ $1,500 spectators = $18,000
- $6,400 costs = $11,600 profit
```

## Financial Best Practices

### Must-Do Financial Habits

1. **Know your break-even number**
   - Calculate before pricing
   - Adjust if needed

2. **Track actual vs. budget**
   - Compare after every event
   - Look for variances

3. **Build cash reserve**
   - Save 10% of profits
   - For emergencies/cancellations

4. **Separate business/personal**
   - Business account
   - Track all expenses
