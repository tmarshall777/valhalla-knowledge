# Chapter 6: Staffing Requirements

## The Foundation: Why Staffing Matters

Poor staffing is the #1 cause of tournament problems. Not bad luck. Not unexpected issues. Just not enough people to get the job done right.

Here's the truth: **It's better to have too many staff than too few.**

An extra table worker costs you $100. Running late because you're understaffed costs you thousands in future revenue.

## Referees

### How Many Do You Need?

- **Minimum:** 1 per mat
- **Better:** 1.25 per mat (extra for breaks)
- **Best:** 1.5 per mat (rotation, emergencies)

### Referee Requirements

- State-certified (required for official events)
- High school or college level experience
- Understand the rules
- Can work the full day

### Paying Referees

| Region | Typical Rate |
|--------|-------------|
| East Coast | $25-35/hour |
| Midwest | $20-30/hour |
| South | $20-30/hour |
| West | $25-35/hour |

- Half-day minimum typical
- Include lunch
- Payment within 48 hours post-event

### Finding Referees

1. Contact state officials association
2. Reach out to local high school coaches
3. Post on wrestling officials forums
4. Previous event refs (re-hire reliable ones)

## Table Workers

### Role Description

**Scorer:**
- Enters match results in TrackWrestling
- Tracks points during match
- Calls matches to table

**Runner:**
- Fetches next wrestlers from staging area
- Takes wrestlers to correct mat
- Returns wrestlers to staging after match

### Training Requirements

From Tim's actual instructions:

> "We will provide training on Sunday morning so you are comfortable. You do not need to memorize the scoring system, the referee will direct scoring and the computer system will guide you."

See Chapter 13 for full table worker training guide.

### Paying Table Workers

| Role | Typical Rate |
|------|-------------|
| Scorer | $15-20/hour |
| Runner | $12-15/hour |
| Head table | $20-25/hour |

- Minimum 4-hour shift
- Provide meals
- Thank them (they're essential)

## Floor Manager

### The Key Role

The floor manager is your **eyes and ears** during the event. They:
- Handle disputes
- Coordinate refs/table workers
- Make real-time decisions
- Communicate with TD

### Floor Manager Responsibilities

- Resolve bracket errors on the spot
- Handle parent/coach complaints
- Coordinate match calls
- Manage timing
- Make "game time" decisions

### Key Qualities Needed

- Experience running events
- Can make quick decisions
- Knows wrestling rules
- Can handle confrontation calmly

## Staff Coordination

### Pre-Event Staffing Checklist

- [ ] Referee count confirmed
- [ ] Table workers confirmed
- [ ] Floor manager confirmed
- [ ] Weigh-in staff confirmed
- [ ] Registration staff confirmed
- [ ] Backup staff identified

### Day-of Staff Meeting

Before the event starts, gather all staff for 15-minute meeting:

1. Welcome and thank yous
2. Review schedule
3. Assign positions
4. Emergency procedures
5. Break schedule
6. Who to go to with questions (floor manager)

## Building a Reliable Team

### The Staff Retention Strategy

The best staff comes back year after year. Here's how to keep them:

**1. Pay fairly and on time**
- Don't be the TD who pays late
- Include meals and snacks

**2. Show appreciation**
- Thank them personally
- Publicly recognize good work
- Small perks (parking, food, swag)

**3. Communicate clearly**
- Give clear instructions
- Set expectations
- Provide feedback

**4. Make it easy**
- Clear schedule
- Easy check-in
- Minimal hassles
