# Chapter 5: Date Selection & Scheduling

## Picking the Right Date

Your date sets everything in motion. Pick wrong, and no amount of management saves you.

### The Golden Rules of Date Selection

1. **Avoid major conflicts**
   - State tournaments
   - National events
   - Holidays
   - School events

2. **Check other tournaments**
   - Look at TrackWrestling for your region
   - Don't compete against established events
   - Give 2-3 week buffer from similar events

3. **Consider your audience**
   - School schedules (fall break, spring break)
   - Holiday weekends (some want them, some don't)
   - Sports seasons (basketball, football overlap)

4. **Weather backup**
   - Have a backup date if outdoor venues
   - Consider winter weather for travel

## Session Planning

How many sessions per day? Here's how to decide:

### Factors to Consider

| Factor | Single Session | Multi-Session |
|--------|----------------|---------------|
| **Wrestlers** | Under 200 | Over 200 |
| **Mats available** | 8+ | Fewer than 6 |
| **Time of day** | Single is easier | Split for timing |
| **Divisions** | Fewer divisions | Many divisions |
| **Venue constraints** | 1 session day | Multiple days |

### The Multi-Session Framework

| Session | Divisions | Start Time | Approximate Duration |
|---------|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| **Morning** | 6U, 10U | 9:00 AM | 3-4 hours |
| **Noon** | 8U, 12U | 12:00 PM | 3-4 hours |
| **Evening** | 15U, HS | 3:00 PM | 3-4 hours |

**Note:** This is Tim's Valhalla format. Adjust based on your event size.

## Weigh-In Windows

How long should weigh-ins be? Here's the formula:

### Standard Formula

```
(# of wrestlers) ÷ (mats × bouts/hour) = minutes needed for weigh-in
```

### Example

- 100 wrestlers in session
- 4 mats running
- 6 bouts/mat/hour = 24 matches/hour
- Need ~4-5 hours of match time
- Add 1 hour for weigh-in = 5-6 hour session

### Typical Windows

| Division Size | Weigh-In Window |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Small (under 50) | 30 minutes |
| Medium (50-100) | 45-60 minutes |
| Large (100+) | 60-90 minutes |

## Start Time Strategy

### The Morning Advantage

- Parents can get kids home by early afternoon
- Less "sitting around" for wrestlers
- Convention centers cheaper in mornings
- Staff fresher

### The Afternoon/Evening Advantage

- Better for families who travel
- Morning setup time more relaxed
- Some wrestlers perform better later

### What's Right for Your Event?

Consider:
- Who is your audience?
- What's the typical start time for similar events?
- What does your venue allow?

## Managing Multiple Divisions

### The Session Assignment Formula

1. **Group by size**
   - Small divisions → can combine
   - Large divisions → need separate sessions

2. **Group by age**
   - Younger divisions often in morning (parents prefer)
   - Older divisions can handle later sessions

3. **Consider weigh-in logistics**
   - Can't have 15U weighing in at 7AM if they're in afternoon session
