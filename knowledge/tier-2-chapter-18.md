# Chapter 18: Weigh-In Systems

## Why Weigh-Ins Matter

Weigh-ins are often where tournaments lose control. Get this wrong, and your entire day is chaotic.

This chapter covers three weigh-in systems: Traditional, Virtual, and Satellite.

---

## Traditional On-Site Weigh-Ins

### The Standard Approach

Wrestlers weigh in at the venue, either day-before or day-of.

### Day-Before Weigh-Ins

**Pros:**
- Day-of runs smoother
- More time for corrections
- Less stress for families

**Cons:**
- Staff required night before
- Some wrestlers may not make weight

**Logistics:**
- 1-hour window typical
- Need 2+ scales
- Need volunteers to run
- Record weights in system

### Day-Of Weigh-Ins

**Pros:**
- No extra day commitment
- Can compete at natural weight after cut

**Cons:**
- Creates morning rush
- Limited time for corrections
- Can delay start if problems

**Logistics:**
- Open 60-90 minutes before first session
- Multiple scales needed
- Must close before first match

---

## Virtual Weigh-In

### What Is Virtual Weigh-In?

Wrestlers weigh in at their own facility, verified via video call.

### How It Works

1. Wrestler registers for virtual weigh-in
2. Receives appointment time (Google Meet, Zoom)
3. At scheduled time, joins video call
4. Shows weight on certified scale
5. Staff verifies and records
6. Wrestler receives verification

### Valhalla's Virtual Weigh-In (From Real Experience)

**Requirements:**
- Must weigh at school/team facility
- Must use certified scale (no bathroom scales)
- Must be in singlet
- Must have Google Meet app
- Max 4 wrestlers per appointment

**Fee:** $20 (covers admin costs)

---

## Satellite Weigh-In

### What Is Satellite?

Wrestlers weigh in at another tournament the day before — official weights transferred to your event.

### How It Works

1. Partner with another tournament
2. Wrestlers weigh in at that event
3. That tournament sends official weights
4. You verify and accept
5. No second weigh-in needed

### Best Practices

- Only accept verified official weights
- Get weights in writing (email)
- Have backup if transfer fails

## Weigh-In Best Practices

### The Weigh-In Station

| Item | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Certified scale | Required |
| Backup scale | Recommended |
| Table | For processing |
| Volunteers | 2-4 people |
| Forms | Weight sheets |
| Wristbands | For verified wrestlers |

### Processing Flow

1. Wrestler arrives at station
2. Shows ID/confirmation
3. Steps on scale
4. Volunteer records weight
5. Volunteer applies wristband
6. Wrestler clears station

---

## Handling Weight Disputes

### The Protocol

1. **Request for re-weigh:**
   - Allow one re-weigh in singlet only
   - No clothes, no shoes
   - Same scale, same official

2. **Verification:**
   - Check original entry
   - Check scale accuracy
   - Document all weigh-ins

3. **Decision:**
   - Floor manager makes final call
   - If disputed weight confirmed, wrestler placed accordingly
   - Document everything

## Weigh-In Summary

| System | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Traditional (day-before)** | Smooth day-of | Extra day |
| **Traditional (day-of)** | No extra trip | Morning rush |
| **Virtual** | No travel | Requires tech |
| **Satellite** | Convenient | Requires partner |
