# Chapter 4: Venue Selection & Negotiation

## Finding the Right Facility

Your venue makes or breaks your tournament. Get it wrong, and no amount of good management will save the day.

### Minimum Venue Requirements

Before you book ANY venue, ensure it meets these minimums:

| Requirement | Why It Matters |
|-------------|----------------|
| **6+ mats** | Competition needs mats to run efficiently |
| **500+ spectator capacity** | Families need to watch |
| **Adequate parking** | 300+ cars for 300+ wrestlers + fans |
| **Climate control** | Wrestlers cut weight in heat = dangerous |
| **WiFi capability** | TrackWrestling needs connectivity |
| **Concessions allowed** | Revenue stream |
| **Proximity to hotels** | For out-of-town families |
| **ADA compliance** | Required by law |

## The Venue Checklist

When touring a potential venue, walk through this:

### Facility Basics
- [ ] Insurance requirements (get certificate ahead of time)
- [ ] Fire Marshall requirements (sometimes needs inspection)
- [ ] EMT/Trainer on-site or required?
- [ ] Police/Security needed?
- [ ] Is outside food/beverage allowed?
- [ ] Concessions — can you run your own?
- [ ] Is janitorial staff included?
- [ ] Is there a cleanup fee?

### AV & Technology
- [ ] Charge for AV (A/V system)?
- [ ] Charge for Internet/WiFi?
- [ ] Is WiFi strong enough for live results?

### Seating & Setup
- [ ] Bleacher seating available?
- [ ] Medal stand available?
- [ ] Backdrop for medal photos?
- [ ] Multiple backdrop areas for photos?
- [ ] Entryway signage space?

### Logistics
- [ ] Mat tape allowed?
- [ ] Table workers area?
- [ ] Additional staff rooms?
- [ ] Food for staff?
- [ ] Walkie talkies needed?

## Negotiating Venue Rates

### The Leverage You Have

Venues WANT your business. Here's why:
- You bring 500-1,500 people
- You bring concession revenue
- You bring repeat business

**Negotiate like it.**

### What to Ask For

| Item | Standard Rate | Negotiated Rate |
|------|---------------|-----------------|
| Full day rental | $2,500 | $1,800 |
| Per hour overtime | $300/hr | $150/hr |
| WiFi | $200 | Free |
| Staff room | $200 | Free |
| Cleanup fee | $500 | Waived |

### The Negotiation Strategy

1. **Book off-season** — Fall/winter rates are lower
2. **Multi-year deals** — Commit to 2-3 years for discounts
3. **Referrals** — "I'll refer other events to you"
4. **Bundle** — Include multiple events for better rates
5. **Ask** — The worst they say is no

## Venue Contract Essentials

### What MUST Be in Writing

- Date and time block (setup through teardown)
- Total cost and payment schedule
- Cancellation policy (what if event is cancelled?)
- Overtime rates
- Insurance requirements (yours and theirs)
- Setup/teardown responsibilities
- What happens if venue fails to deliver

### The Cancellation Clause

Include:
- 60-day notice for no penalty
- 30-day notice = 50% deposit forfeited
- Less than 30 days = full payment due
- Venue cancellation = full refund + 20%

## Venue Walkthrough (Pre-Booking)

Before you sign, DO a walkthrough:

### Week of Event
- Confirm date/time
- Review setup layout
- Confirm electrical outlets
- Check WiFi strength
- Test PA system

### Day Before
- Venue access confirmed
- Final headcount
- Setup timeline reviewed

### Issues to Catch Early
- "We thought you were bringing tables"
- "The WiFi password changed"
- "The heating isn't working"
- "Someone else has the room"

| **Rural** | National Guard armories, community centers |
