# Chapter 19: Volunteer Management

## Volunteers Are the Backbone

You can't run a tournament without help. But managing volunteers is a skill. Get it right, and they keep coming back. Get it wrong, and you'll never see them again.

---

## Where Volunteers Fit

### Volunteer Roles vs. Paid Staff

| Role Type | Typical Roles |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Paid** | Referees, head table |
| **Volunteer** | Table workers, runners, registration, awards |

Most events use volunteers for:
- Table workers
- Runners
- Registration help
- Awards distribution
- Concessions

---

## Recruiting Volunteers

### Where to Find Them

1. **Wrestling families**
   - Parents often want to help
   - Even better: require volunteer hours
   - Good fit for team fundraising

2. **Local high schools**
   - Student volunteers
   - Community service hours
   - Good for concession sales

3. **College programs**
   - College wrestlers looking for experience
   - May work for experience/letter

4. **Community service groups**
   - Boy Scouts, Girl Scouts
   - National Honor Society
   - Local clubs

### Recruiting Strategies

- **At your own events:** Ask parents at registration
- **Through clubs:** Partner with local clubs for volunteers
- **Social media:** Post call for volunteers
- **School networks:** Connect with athletic departments

---

## Training Volunteers

### Basic Training Requirements

For table workers specifically:

1. **What to expect** (from Tim's actual instructions)
   - This will be easy to learn
   - Training provided morning-of
   - You don't need to know wrestling rules

2. **Key procedures:**
   - Identify each wrestler (red vs green)
   - Click save result
   - Never use back button
   - Report errors to head table

3. **Communication:**
   - Mat Chat for calling wrestlers
   - Where to stand
   - When to take breaks

### Training Day-of

- 15-minute briefing
- Assign position
- Show what to do
- Have them shadow experienced worker

## Scheduling Volunteers

### Volunteer Shift Guidelines

- **Maximum shift:** 4-6 hours
- **Break:** 30 minutes required
- **Water/snacks:** Always available
- **Designated break area:** Required

### Creating Shift Schedule

| Time Block | Position | Volunteer Count |
|------------|----------|-----------------|
| 7:30-12:00 | Morning session | 6-8 |
| 12:00-5:00 | Afternoon session | 6-8 |
| 5:00-close | Evening session | 4-6 |

---

## Volunteer Retention

### How to Keep Them Coming Back

**1. Treat them well**
- Thank them sincerely
- Feed them (meals included)
- Appreciate their time

**2. Make it easy**
- Clear instructions
- Minimal hassle
- Know their role before arrival

**3. Recognize contribution**
- Public thank yous
- Social media shoutouts
- Small swag (t-shirt, sticker)

**4. Provide value**
- Community connection
- Resume builder for students
- Good experience
